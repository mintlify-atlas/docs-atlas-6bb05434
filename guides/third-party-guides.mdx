The Swift community has produced a wide range of learning resources beyond the official documentation. This page organizes them by format so you can pick the style that matches how you learn best.

## Video and courses

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card
    title="Developing iOS Apps with Swift (Stanford)"
    icon="graduation-cap"
    href="https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/developing-ios-11-apps-with-swift/id1315130780"
  >
    Paul Hegarty's legendary Stanford CS193p course, available as a free podcast. It covers Swift and UIKit from first principles and is widely regarded as one of the most rigorous free iOS development courses available.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Conferences.digital"
    icon="video"
    href="https://github.com/zagahr/Conferences.digital"
  >
    A native macOS app that lets you watch Swift and iOS conference talks. Instead of hunting through YouTube, you get a curated library of conference videos in a purpose-built player.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Ray Wenderlich / Kodeco Tutorials"
    icon="play"
    href="https://www.kodeco.com"
  >
    High-quality tutorials, videos, podcasts, and books covering Swift, SwiftUI, UIKit, and the broader Apple platform ecosystem. One of the most comprehensive third-party learning platforms for Apple developers.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Hands-on and interactive

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card
    title="30 Days of Swift"
    icon="calendar"
    href="https://github.com/allenwong/30DaysofSwift"
  >
    A project-a-day challenge that walks you through building 30 small Swift apps in 30 days. A practical way to build coding habits and explore the language through real, runnable examples.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Hacking with Swift"
    icon="hammer"
    href="https://www.hackingwithswift.com"
  >
    A free, complete training course that teaches app development through 30 hands-on projects. Paul Hudson's approachable writing style and breadth of coverage — from SwiftUI basics to advanced topics — make this one of the most-recommended free resources in the community.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="About Swift (Playground)"
    icon="swift"
    href="https://github.com/NicolaLancellotti/about-swift"
  >
    An Xcode Playground that covers Swift language features interactively. Open it in Xcode, run the code, and explore how the language behaves — no separate project setup needed.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Swift & SwiftUI Tutorials"
    icon="square-code"
    href="http://ww1.janeshswift.com"
  >
    A collection of SwiftUI learning materials designed to make picking up the framework approachable, with examples organized for progressive learning.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Written resources and references

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card
    title="SwiftDoc"
    icon="file-text"
    href="https://sosumi.ai/"
  >
    Auto-generated documentation for the Swift standard library. Use it when you need a quick reference for built-in types, protocols, and functions without digging through Xcode's documentation browser.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="SwiftGuide CN"
    icon="book"
    href="https://github.com/ipader/SwiftGuide"
  >
    A comprehensive Swift guide written in Chinese. An essential bookmark for Mandarin-speaking developers looking for curated Swift resources in their primary language.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Awesome Swift Education"
    icon="list"
    href="https://github.com/hsavit1/Awesome-Swift-Education"
  >
    An organized collection of essential Swift language topics, linking out to articles, videos, and talks for each subject area. Useful when you want to go deep on a specific concept.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Tips and community collections

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card
    title="SwiftTips (John Sundell)"
    icon="lightbulb"
    href="https://github.com/JohnSundell/SwiftTips"
  >
    A curated collection of practical Swift tips from John Sundell, creator of the Swift by Sundell blog and podcast. Each tip demonstrates a concise technique or language feature you can apply immediately.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="swift-tips (Vincent Pradeilles)"
    icon="lightbulb"
    href="https://github.com/vincent-pradeilles/swift-tips"
  >
    A series of useful Swift tips by Vincent Pradeilles, covering language features, patterns, and idiomatic approaches to common problems.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Swift Education (Community)"
    icon="users"
    href="https://github.com/swifteducation"
  >
    A GitHub organization run by a community of educators who share materials for teaching Swift and app development. Useful if you are learning in an academic context or preparing to teach Swift yourself.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  If you are just starting out, begin with **Hacking with Swift** or the **Stanford course** for structured learning. Once you are comfortable with the basics, **SwiftTips** and **swift-tips** are great for leveling up your idiomatic Swift.
</Tip>
